The Equation Explained

\(H_{t+1}\) \(=\) \(h\) \(+\) \(r_h\) \(\times\) \(H_t\) \(+\) \(I_{WH}(\) \(W_t\) \()\)

This “equation” is actually a pair of reccuring integer number sequences. Here, a couple is represented by the sequences called \((H_t)\), for Husband, and \((W_t)\), for Wife.
In simple terms, each partner’s next mood state is the sum of their “basic mood”, their previous state, and the influence the other partner has on them.
In the above, only the equation for the husband is written, but the one for the wife is exactly the same: just reverse the H’s and the W’s.

\(H_{t+1}\)
Husband’s next mood state
\(h\)
Husband’s “basic” mood state
\(r_h\)
Husband’s capacity to stay in his mood over time: emotional inertia
\(H_t\)
Husband’s current mood state
\(I_{WH}\)
A mathematical function indicating the wife’s influence on the husband
\(W_t\)
Wife’s current mood state
